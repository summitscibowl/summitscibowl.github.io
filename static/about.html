Summit Science Bowl is a group of college students from across North America, looking to prepare students for the National Science Bowl competition. The material chosen for each course has been selected carefully by the alumni, to maximize the efficiency of improvement.

Geoffrey Wu

Geoffrey Wu is the Head mathematics instructor and a space science instructor. He placed 2nd overall in the 2021 National Science Bowl and top 16 in the 2020 National Science Bowl Competition. He is an IOAA Gold Medalist and 3-time USAMO qualifier, in addition to placing 2nd in the 2017 RAYTHEON Mathcounts National Competition. Geoffrey has also qualified for the USA Physics Olympiad 2 times, including earning a silver medal.

Bill Zhao

Bill Zhao is the lead instructor for chemistry and one of the earth science instructors and placed 4th overall in the 2021 National Science Bowl. He is studying physics, chemistry, and computer science at the University of Florida. He is a USNCO study camp qualifier, USAPhO qualifier, and an AIME qualifier. Bill also has won many prizes at buzzing competitions including LOST and NHSST. He has also playtested and written questions for many online tournaments.

Rohan Shelke

Rohan Shelke is the Head Biology Instructor and one of the Earth Science instructors. He is currently studying Molecular and Cell Biology and Business at UC Berkeley, and has competed in Science Bowl for over 6 years. He has attended the NSB National Finals 3 times as a member of the Mira Loma Science Bowl team, which placed second at the 2020 NSB Nationals. Additionally, Rohan was an alternate for IESO Team USA, and ranked in the Top-50 of USA Biology Olympiad. Rohan has also written for many tournaments including MIT Science Bowl, MOSFET, Prometheus Science Bowl.

Sanjay Suresh

Sanjay is the head physics instructor and one of the Space Science instructors for Summit Scibowl. Sanjay Suresh is a senior in high school and was the captain of the 18th place team at the 2021 National Science Bowl. He will be studying Math, Physics, and Computer Science in the fall at UW-Madison and has competed in Science Bowl for 5 years. He has won numerous prizes in buzzing competitions including placing 4th at the 2021 MIT Invitational Tournament, 5th at LOST 2021, 5th Individually, and 3rd as a team in the National High School Science Tournament. Sanjay also has competed and achieved national-level recognition in olympiads such as USNCO and USAAAO. He is also an AIME qualifier. Sanjay has organized multiple online science bowl tournaments and written and playtested for many other tournaments.
